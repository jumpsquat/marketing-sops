**Level of Awareness (Schwartz):**

- **Solution-aware, trending toward Product-aware.** The prospect already knows (a) they have a body-held / energetic / somatic problem, and (b) that things like breathwork, meditation, community, and "energy work" exist as solutions. What they don't know is *which* container is trustworthy and *whether Dr. Lee is the one*. The top of the funnel (masterclass) moves them from Solution-aware to Product-aware; the sales page converts the now-Product-aware prospect.
- Implication: the page doesn't need to sell the *category* — it needs to sell Dr. Lee as the right guide and ELT as the right container. Credentialing, discovery story, community size, and pricing anchor do the work.

**Stage of Sophistication (Schwartz):**

- **Stage 4 / moving to Stage 5.** The holistic-wellness, awakening, and trauma-healing markets are extremely crowded (Dispenza, Maté, Hyman, Mindvalley, countless breathwork and plant-medicine operators). Claims of "heal your trauma" and "activate your potential" are commoditized. Prospects are jaded.
- ELT's Stage 5 play: *make it about the identity of the buyer, not the mechanism itself*. "You are a Tribe member. You belong with the people who get it. You already know you're awakening — this is the home for it." Pair that with Dr. Lee's unique positioning (psychiatrist who left the system) to create a bespoke angle nobody else can copy.

Apps address habits. *Nobody* addresses all four simultaneously under a single framework, so the healing never compounds — each modality undoes or competes with the others. On top of that, modern psychiatry has been taught to ignore 90% of the human: the epigenetic, somatic, energetic, and communal layers. The prospect has been failed not by a lack of effort but by a *category error in how healing is organized*.

The "DNA activation" language is the *unifying metaphor* that stitches all four layers together under one narrative — the thing a fractured marketplace has never offered.
